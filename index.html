About Me

AI Researcher at INRS Montreal

I am an AI researcher specializing in Large Language Models (LLMs), Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), and trustworthy artificial intelligence. My work focuses on improving the robustness, reliability, and safety of LLM systems using knowledge graphs, advanced retrieval methods, and multilingual NLP.

I am a published author in IEEE conferences and international journals, with research contributions in:

  • Knowledge Graph-enhanced RAG (GraphRAG)
  • Misinformation detection using LLMs
  • AI robustness and hallucination mitigation
  • Ideological bias analysis in LLMs
  • Multilingual NLP and cross-lingual retrieval

My goal is to develop production-ready, trustworthy AI systems for real-world applications.

Research Projects

GraphRAG: Knowledge Graph-Enhanced Retrieval-Augmented Generation

Designed and implemented a Knowledge Graph-enhanced Retrieval-Augmented Generation (GraphRAG) framework to improve factual accuracy, robustness, and reasoning of Large Language Models. Evaluated performance using the RGB benchmark under noise robustness, counterfactual robustness, and negative rejection scenarios.
